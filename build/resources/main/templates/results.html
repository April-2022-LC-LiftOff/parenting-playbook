Impulse Control

Impulse control is the ability to “stop and think” before making a choice or completing an action.

Difficulties with impulse control may look like…

  • interrupting
  • shouting out/blurting out
  • saying or doing things impulsively
  • struggling to determine how their actions will impact others while in the moment

    Emotional Control

    Emotional control is the ability to self-regulate one’s emotions.

    Difficulties with emotional control may look like…

  • screaming/yelling
  • tantrumming
  • lashing out when upset
  • crying frequently
  • changing emotions -seemingly going from "0 to 100" quickly
  • inability to determine the size of a problem in the moment
    		•
    Flexible Thinking

    Flexible thinking is the ability to “go with the flow” and adapt to situations by changing the plan or strategy.

    Difficulties with flexible thinking may look like…

  • rigidity
  • unable to cope with changes in routine
  • anxiety when plans change unexpectedly
  • refusal or hesitance to try other strategies when their strategy isn't working
    		•
    Working Memory

    Working memory is a person’s ability to retain information in order to complete a task.

    Difficulties with working memory may look like…

  • inability to follow multiple-step directions
  • forgetting directions immediately after hearing them
  • struggles with following the steps to complete an assignment
  • quickly forgetting what others have said to them
    		•
    Self-Monitoring

    Self-monitoring is a person’s ability to check in on themselves and evaluate how they are doing when they are working on a task.

    Difficulties with self-monitoring may look like…

  • becoming easily distracted
  • struggles with or an inability to complete a task without prompting
  • getting off-topic when working on an assignment
  • not demonstrating age-appropriate learning behaviors (e.g. working quietly, staying on the assignment/web page, etc.)
    		•
    Planning and Prioritizing/Time Management

    Planning and prioritizing/Time Management are the mental processes that allow people to make a plan of action and stick to it.

    Difficulties with planning and prioritizing/time management may look like…

  • often being too late or too early to events or appearing to be "time blind" (unaware of timing)
  • inability to intuitively plan out how long something will take to complete
  • inability to keep or follow a to-do list in order of priority
  • struggles with identifying the steps to complete a task in a logical order
    		•
    Task Initiation

    Task initiation is the ability to start a task, stick with it, and avoid procrastination.

    Difficulties with task initiation may look like…

  • procrastinating
  • saying "I don't know where/how to start."
  • getting side-tracked or easily distracted
  • struggles with sitting down and getting to work
    		•
    Organization

    Organization is the ability for a person to create or utilize systems to manage their belongings and thoughts.

    Difficulties with organization may look like…

  • having piles of items that aren't put away
  • cluttered areas or a messy desk/bedroom
  • thoughts and writing are shared out of order or in a seemingly random order
  • unable to independently create and maintain organizational systems
  • struggles with understanding how to keep a space organized and neat
